Bio

I am a PhD student in Statistics at Trinity College Dublin (TCD), working under the supervision of Dr Jason Wyse in the School of Computer Science & Statistics.

My research focuses on sequential Bayesian inference for infectious disease modelling. I develop and apply particle filtering (Sequential Monte Carlo) methods for real-time estimation of transmission dynamics from epidemic data, with an emphasis on robust, computationally efficient algorithms for state-space epidemic models.

My recent work includes developing SMC² methods for online inference in stochastic epidemic models, a Lifebelt Particle Filter for robust inference in SEIR models, and ensemble data assimilation approaches for sequential Bayesian inference. I also maintain an interest in combining mechanistic epidemic models (SEIR) with statistical point-process models (Hawkes processes).

Prior to my PhD, I completed a Master's in Mathematical Sciences (Data Science track) at the African Institute for Mathematical Sciences (AIMS), Rwanda, graduating with Distinction, and a Master's in Applied Mathematics at the University of Dschang, Cameroon. I also held a Pre-Doctoral Oxford Scholarship through the Mfano-Africa programme at the Mathematical Institute, University of Oxford.
